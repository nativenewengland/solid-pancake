Overview

The harpy aeries of the Screaming Fells perch along jagged basaltic ridges that punch through the cloud sea. Their communal nests are suspended on braided stormvine anchors that hum in the wind, doubling as both musical heralds and seismic alarms. Fells harpies rely on seasonal migrations between the thunder-drenched Westspire and the calmer southern mesas, timing each relocation with the arrival of lightning-fed thermal currents.

Contrary to popular tavern tales, harpy society is not a matriarchal tyranny but a lattice of interdependent song-clans. These clans share rookeries and rotate stewardship of the windwell cisterns that condense drinkable fog. Outsiders petitioning for passage are expected to contribute ballast salts or the loan of sky charts before being granted a gliding escort.

Aerie Hierarchies

Each aerie is composed of three braided circles that balance responsibility for flight, memory, and sustenance. Leadership rotates every equinox to ensure no single voice dominates the chorus that governs migration or warflight decisions.

Thermocline Circle

Scouts and windcallers who map updraft corridors and patrol trade routes. They conduct nightly beacon sweeps with mirrored talons to warn tethered caravans of shear winds.

Echo Circle

Lorekeepers who memorize stormballads, adjudicate disputes, and coordinate harmonic rites that steady fledglings during their first descent to the valley markets.

Ember Circle

Harvesters who cultivate cliffside embermoss, process cloud milk, and oversee the exchange of guststone batteries with human aerostat guilds.

Windborne Tactics

Harpy warflights emphasize disruption over annihilation. Wing pairs weave through stormfronts to blind siege crews with flares of powdered quicklime, while heavier talon cadres puncture airship envelopes using obsidian harpoons tethered to magnetic reels.

Doctrine Addendum

The 3rd Aerial Legion recorded that the harpy offensive tempo falters when thunderheads are absent. Strategists recommend timing parley offers for dawn calms, when Thermocline scouts must roost to recalibrate their barometric chimes.

Song-Lore & Rituals

Song is both law and ledger in harpy culture. Harmonized trilling recounts ancestral flights, while deep-throat drones codify trade oaths. Outsiders attempting to sing without formal invitation risk signaling debt or duel challenges.

Fledglings receive their first featherbraid—a woven record of kinship—after completing the Cloudstay Vigil, a night spent suspended beneath the rookery with only a low-burning embermoss lantern for warmth.

Sky Trade

Harpies monopolize the transport of fresh thunderstone cores, negotiating delivery to mountain keeps in exchange for salt, polished glass, and finely tuned gliders. Their pact with the River Guild ensures annual grain drops onto the upper mesas.

Merchants seeking their favor typically commission windproof ledgers bound in sky-silk. The offer of warming coils is viewed as a grave insult, implying the aerie cannot maintain its own thermal drafts.
